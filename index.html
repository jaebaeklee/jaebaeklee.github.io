Lend a helping hand

Breaking Bread Metro Dallas is partnering with the 8/11 Project to engage the community by volunteering to help cook and serve meals as well as to provide financial support for our organization. Interested parties can sponsor a meal for our participants while volunteering in our kitchen. Our impact goal is to garner more than 1,500 volunteers to help feed the homeless and raise more than $350,000.
‍
Each month, program participants are served 2 meals - one natural and one spiritual. Groups from corporations, civic and professional associations, high schools, universities and the faith-based community have the opportunity to sponsor, cook and serve a meal which feeds 144 men and women who have transitioned from homeless shelter, are disabled, or low income.

Give back to your community

Call To Action
When members of our community break bread with our participants, we begin to break barriers in the fight against hunger and homelessness. Breaking Bread helps us test the core components of a strategic, multi-phase development plan.

Have fun!

Call To Action
Experience the personal satisfactino of knowing you made a difference - it will be an experience you will always remember.

For those transitioning from homelessness, the simple act of finding a meal can be insurmountable. For many families and individuals, a communal meal serves as a source of healing and connecting with others. The 8/11 Project Food Service Program meets these needs by providing nutritional well-balanced meals for residents, including the provision of special dietary plans for the in firmed and elderly.
